Grip'n'Hold

return to engineering

Task: To design a device that allows the user to hold an umbrella without their hands.

Skills: collaboration, use of design process, prototyping, machine shop

Final Product:

The final prototype comprised of an adjustable 2-part device that attaches to a backpack. The cylindrical bottom holds the base of the umbrella, and the upper gripper allows for increased stability and security to keep the umbrella upright.


Defining the Problem:

My team was designing for a stroke survivor who is paralyzed on his left side and holds a cane in his right hand.

User Observation:

We worked with the user's occupational therapists at the Shirley Ryan AbilityLab to observe the user doing everyday tasks and activities. One key observation was that he always carried a backpack.

Brainstorm and Ideate:

We then came up with 3 different mockups that each worked in a different way, each of them designed with the backpack in mind.

User Testing:

Mockup tests with the user showed pain points through the way the user interacted with the mockups. One idea was to mimic a backpack’s water bottle pocket with the device, but when testing with the user, his shoulders were wider than ours and the umbrella did not fully cover him.




Prototyping:

User testing results clearly showed our team's best mockup. Next steps were to optimize the mockup’s shapes and materials. We added in rubber to the top component to better grip the umbrella.

Future Steps:

Further development of the prototype would be to test the prototype on different backpacks and umbrellas. Because this was made specifically for the user we were working with, certain things were tailored more to his height and build. Additionally, we can simplify the design and make it sturdier and more aesthetically pleasing.
