Toy Manufacturing Mold

return to engineering

Objective: Take a concept provided by the partner grade school classrooms, and develop a product involving an assembly of two or more molded plastic parts.

  • Design of moldable part (sketch and CAD model)
  • Selection of assembly method (snap fit vs. interference fit)
  • Generate manufacturing program on Siemens NX with CAM operations to machine molds
  • Tooling design and machining of mold on CNC machine
  • Injection-mold the parts and assemble

Skills: computer-aided design (CAD), computer-aided manufacturing (CAM) programming, cnc machine, injection-molder, product development

Main issues: When injection molding the part, the main issue we came across was flash of the molten plastic. We had to do several test-runs to figure out the timing of the injection so there wouldn't be any flashes or short-shots.


Phase 1: Part Design

Sketches: The initial sketch was a shark rocket with a mushroom hat. Our team decided to split it up into 3 different parts with snap fits: the mushroom hat, front of the shark body, and the back of the shark body.

Phase 2: Mold Design

We used mold blank CAD files and then created the core and cavity molds using our CAD design. After that, we used the manufacturing processes tab of NX to create the CNC program.

Then, we loaded the code onto the CNC and machined the two parts of the mold.



Finally, we injection-molded around 30 rounds and assembled the toys with a snap-fit involving bosses.
